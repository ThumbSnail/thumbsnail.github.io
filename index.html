Climby Charts: Buried Alive in the App Heap

Inspired by my first two experiences with the app store, I created this humorous game about how hard it is for a developer to get noticed at all. You have to dodge Flappy Bird clones and one-star reviews on your futile quest to collect downloads and climb the charts.

Switching over to Unity for this project meant learning some of the differences between C# and C++. It also meant discovering ways to deal with the bloated excess that comes with using a 3D engine to develop a 2D game. ("No, Unity, Climby Charts does NOT need to be 100 MB!")

Try it out on iOS or on Android through Amazon. Or for more info, check out my blog.

Super Hearts: Double Deck Cancellation Chaos

For my second game, I really wanted to reuse all the art assets I had made and even some of the code. So this time it was the classic card game "Hearts" but with a double deck and the interesting strategies that arise from being able to cancel a paired card.

The most challenging aspect about designing this game was creating the seven different AI difficulties. "Hearts" isn't a simple, tidy game like "Sevens" where you know the best solution right from the beginning. The AIs here aren't as perfect as I'd like, but I think they ended up being worthy opponents. And most importantly, they're just a lot of fun.

You can try it out for free on Android through Amazon Underground. Or for more info, check out my blog.

Super Sevens

This is the first game I ever made (if you don't count Visual Basic Pong in high school)! I took the classic card game "Sevens" and spiced it up by allowing you to play with up to 10 different suits, opponents, and suit lengths, as well as 7 different difficulties. And a double deck. And a survivor/hearts-style mode.

I learned a ton through this, and it's impossible to list everything. I learned C++. I learned how to use a framework (Cocos2d-x). I learned how to search the internet for answers to all kinds of programming questions and problems. I even learned about feature creep, including the wholly unnecessary addition of an SQLite database to track gameplay statistics.

For more information, check out my blog.
